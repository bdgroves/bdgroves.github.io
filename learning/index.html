The Amsterdam & London Logs

Nine days, two cities, and one 50th birthday. From the canals of Amsterdam to a flat in Chelsea — a medieval convent turned six-hundred-year-old hotel, a giant swing hanging a hundred meters over the IJ, a food tour through the Dutch Golden Age, the water pump where modern epidemiology was born, a power station famous for a runaway pig, and an exclusive London club with a fox on the lawn. There were four World Cup matches that resolved somewhere over Greenland, a beer arc that started resigned and ended rewarded, and one castle robe that remains, to this day, unaccounted for. This is the whole logbook.

The Trip in Brief

The route: Seattle 🇺🇸 → Amsterdam 🇳🇱 — five days of canals, Van Gogh, and the last day of Aimee's forties — then London 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿, four days in Chelsea with old friends, and home over the North Atlantic chasing the sunset.

The cast: me and Aimee — the clever fox 🦊, who turned fifty and celebrated it dangling off a building. Our hosts David & Michele in Chelsea; Barney the Amsterdam food-tour guide; a Greek-Bulgarian bartender-philosopher; and a boatful of pin-gifting Australians.

The running threads: the fox, the beer arc, purple drinks in two cities, a World Cup that threaded through the whole trip, Pink Floyd's Animals from Battersea to the flight home, two Amazing Race airport sprints as bookends — and, told to us near the very end, the Case of the Missing Robe.
