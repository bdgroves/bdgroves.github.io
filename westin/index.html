Stories from the Westin Seattle

Bell captain dispatches from a 900-room skyscraper of hospitality. Graveyard shifts, radio codes, VIP secrets, and the strange physics of hotel life. Twenty-one years of stories. These are some of them.

Westin Seattle · 1997–2018 · Call sign: 940

The stories collected here are based on the author's personal recollections from twenty-one years employed at a large hotel in downtown Seattle. All guest names have been omitted or changed. Colleague names and identifying details have been altered. Certain events have been compressed or reordered for narrative clarity. The hotel itself is identifiable and intentionally so — the institution is part of the story. Nothing here is intended to disparage any individual, living or dead. These are one person's memories of a particular place and time, told as honestly as memory allows.
